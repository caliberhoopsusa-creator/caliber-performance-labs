1. Player Profile & “Basketball Resume”

Make every player feel like they’re building their 2K MyPlayer card in real life.

Add:

Season averages & best games

Custom player card (photo, position, height, class, strengths)

Shareable profile link (for socials, recruiting, group chats)

“Top 5 Games” section

Why it helps:
If a kid can post a clean-looking “HoopReport Card” on IG / X / Snapchat, they’re marketing your app for you.

2. Team & Coach Mode

If you win over coaches, you get entire teams… and teams bring leagues.

Add:

Coach accounts that can:

Create teams

Add players

Enter stats for the whole roster

View team dashboards (grades, effort, trends)

Export/print report cards for film sessions or parent meetings

Simple import from stat sheets / box score CSVs

Why it helps:
A coach saying, “Everyone download HoopReport, we’re using this all season,” is way more powerful than you trying to get every kid one-by-one.

3. Leaderboards & Rankings (Local → National)

Hoopers LOVE seeing where they stack up.

Add:

Leaderboards by:

School

City

State

Class year

Position

Filters: “Offense grade”, “Defense grade”, “Hustle grade”, “Most improved”

Weekly “HoopReport Player of the Week” badges

Why it helps:
Competition + ego = free growth. People will invite teammates and friends just to climb the ranks or prove they’re better.

4. Highlight Integration & Auto-Graphics

Make the app part of their content, not just their stats.

Add:

Ability to:

Upload one game highlight link (YouTube, Hudl, etc.)

Attach it to that game’s report

Auto-generate a shareable graphic:

Player photo

Grade (A-)

Key stats

QR code / link to full HoopReport profile

Why it helps:
The more your logo and grades are in screenshots on people’s stories, the faster this spreads.

5. Progress, Goals & Training

Turn it into a development tool, not just a stat calculator.

Add:

Player-set goals:

e.g., “Average B+ or higher on Defense by March”

“Reduce turnovers below 2 per game”

Streak tracking:

“3 games in a row with A or better in Effort”

Drill suggestions tied to weaknesses (you already started this):

Missed a lot of FTs? Show FT routine drills.

Low hustle score? Show rebounding / conditioning drills.

Why it helps:
Parents and serious hoopers will stick with a tool that clearly shows improvement over time, not just one-time grades.

6. “Compare Yourself To…” Mode

Every kid wants to know, “Who do I play like?”

Add:

A page that compares their stat profile to:

Typical college guard/wing/big benchmarks

Play style archetypes: “3&D Wing”, “Slashing Guard”, “Stretch Big”, etc.

A fun label:

“Your profile is similar to a ‘Two-way Slasher’”

“You’re grading like a high-motor glue guy”

Why it helps:
This makes the app fun and shareable (“Yo, it says I’m like a 3&D guard 😂”).

7. Referral / Team Challenges

This is straight growth-hacking.

Add:

Referral system:

“Invite 3 teammates, unlock advanced charts or custom card themes”

Team challenges:

“If 10+ players from one school use the app in a week → unlock your school badge / theme”

Monthly “Program Leaderboard”:

Which school logs the most games?

Why it helps:
You bake virality into the product itself, not just rely on luck.

8. Multi-Game & Season Insights

Right now, you’re analyzing single games. Now think season story.

Add:

“Last 5 games” trend charts for each category

“Biggest jump” (e.g., Hustle up 12 points over the last month)

Flags like:

“You’ve improved your Efficiency score from 72 → 84 over the last 10 games.”

“Defense has dropped in your last 3 games – possible fatigue or matchups?”

Why it helps:
Serious hoopers and coaches LOVE trend data. It feels like real analytics, not just a calculator.

9. Parents & Recruiter View

Two more audiences who can blow this up: parents and eventually scouts.

Add:

Parent-friendly summary:

Simple explanation: “Johnny is a B+ overall, with A- effort and B defense.”

“Recruiting resume” export:

PDF or web link with:

Photo, bio

GPA (if they add it)

Season stats & grades

Honors (POW badges, etc.)

Why it helps:
If parents see this as a helpful, legit-looking evaluation, they’ll encourage their kids, coaches, and club teams to use it.

10. Make It Stupid Easy to Use

If you want everyone to use it, you’ve gotta remove friction.

Add:

Presets / templates:

“Import from basic box score” (just PTS, REB, AST, STL, BLK, TO, FG, FT)

Advanced mode if someone has full stats

Save stat templates for teams so coaches don’t have to re-enter everything.

Auto-calc some stats (REB = OREB + DREB if they forget one, etc.).

Why it helps:
If entering stats is annoying, no one uses it long enough for it to spread.

TL;DR — If You Had to Pick Just a Few Killer Adds

If you want to prioritize, I’d start with:

Player profiles + shareable game cards

Team/coach mode + team dashboards

Leaderboards + rankings

Progress charts + goals

Those four alone make it:

Fun for players

Useful for coaches

Impressive to parents

Naturally shareable online
